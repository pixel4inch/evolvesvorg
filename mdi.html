WHAT'S TRENDING

Innovation holds an important place in today’s highly competitive business environment and the global healthcare market is no exception to this. Innovations have a disruptive impact on the overall medical device industry. Generally advancements in the medtech space result from significant collaborations, where the role of contract manufacturers continues to grow in prominence as OEMs increasingly adopt outsourcing strategy for production operations. Advanced technology concepts such as Artificial Intelligence (AI), IoT, Cloud computing, Big Data, and Robotic Process Automation (RPA) are likely to improve and augment the contract manufacturing landscape in the coming years. With precision manufacturing taking center stage in the medical device vertical, technologies that fully support the concept are being increasingly sought by contract manufacturers across the board, while spurring the adoption of AI and RPA powered production lines. The development of innovative medical devices entails significant complexities, and requires tremendous investments in time, efforts and cost on part of the medical device company. Almost all medical devices, regardless of the level of associated complexity, involve several complicated stages starting from idea generation stage to the final launch of the product in the market. Device manufacturers attempt to focus on continuous innovation in technologies to justify higher margin. Rapid technological obsolescence and technical parity within competing devices makes time-to-market critical for most of the device product lines. When technical innovation occurs, older technologies will be used in commodity items while new technologies fuel growth through premium pricing strategies. Therefore, OEMs are accelerating product developments and are treading new paths to get their products to more quickly to market, in order to gain first mover advantages. Drug-device combinations (DDCs), Preventive and predictive healthcare, and self-care, self-management are some of the key focus areas for medical devices looking to launch innovative products.

  • Robotic Process Automation - Robotic Process Automation (RPA) allows end users to easily access information from various sources such as electronic health record software, accounting system, and payer portals. The novel technology assists medical practitioners in streamlining workflow, along with improving employee productivity. Driven by its ability to offer cost-efficient, reliable, and safe solutions, demand for RPA devices is gaining momentum. In order to tap the available opportunity, several leading global companies are now introducing RPA systems under their product portfolio.
  • Customized Healthcare Software- In order to gain competitive advantage, it is critical for healthcare service providers to gain comprehensive understanding regarding constantly evolving customer demands. The advent of Electronic Health Record (EHR), Customer Relationship Management (CRM), and mobile applications are enabling them in fulfilling this requirement. The novel software is assisting healthcare practitioners in storing, accessing, and analyzing a wide range of confidential information. Introduction of the advanced technologies are assisting medical practitioners in streamlining decision making process. Driven by their ability to enhance customer satisfaction, the use of digital healthcare technologies is witnessing a robust surge. Further, due to the ongoing technological advancements, several manufacturers are now focusing their attention towards developing customized healthcare software. Though currently at inception stage of product development, the novel software is expected to streamline process associated with filing insurance claims. Additionally, the advanced system is likely to assist patients in easily reporting errors using various digital pathways. Propelled by their abilities to offer customized, cost-efficient, and holistic solutions, demand for automated healthcare devices is expected to gain momentum.
  • Wearable Healthcare Devices - Advancements in circuit miniaturization technology is enabling companies to design various wearable medical devices including smart glasses, biopatches, and ECG monitors. The advanced technologies allow medical practitioners to constantly monitor patient’s healthcare data from a remote location. Propelled by their ability to enhance prognosis efficiency and patient outcome, the demand for wearable healthcare devices is exhibiting a significant increase. Fuelled by promising outlook, several leading global companies are now making a foray into the highly prospective segment. For instance, Aidmed, a Poland-based company introduced chest-worn portable wearable recorder. The advanced system utilizes biosensors for recording various parameters, including electrocardiogram, temperature, and blood pressure levels. Aidmed uses AI technology for converting and analysing a wide range of critical healthcare related information. Owing to its ability to offer accurate and reliable solutions, the novel system is gaining acceptance among large number of medical practitioners. Going by the trend, Gate Science, a US-based start-up developed RELAY, a pain-controlling wearable equipment. The multimodal device comprises both neuromodulation and pharmacologic blockade features. Gate Science’s RELAY application allows both clinicians and patients to easily control post-surgery pain.
  • Medical Robots - In contrast to conventional laparoscopy, medical robots-assisted surgeries ensure that patient undergo smaller incisions and minimum blood loss. Driven by their abilities to enhance operational efficiency and ergonomics, various hospitals are now using disinfecting robots. Fuelled by high growth prospects, several leading global manufacturers are now entering the highly promising segment. Going by the trend, Punar, an India-based start-up introduced Nucleus, a portable hand muscle training system. The robotic exoskeleton utilizes proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation technology for exercising paralyzed hands. Nucleus constantly generates accurate data pertaining to patient recovery, thereby improving overall rehabilitation process. Similarly, Theranautilus, an India-based company introduced BIO BOTS under its magnetic bots product range. The novel system is specifically designed for treating patients suffering from various oral diseases. Owing to its ability to promote tooth regeneration and cure hypersensitivity, BIO BOTS is gaining acceptance among various dentists.
  • 3D Printing Technology - The advent of 3D printing technology is enabling manufacturers to design cost-efficient and affordable medical equipment. The advanced system is allowing companies to easily perform various pathological and anatomical functions. Owing to their superior features, additive manufacturing is allowing firms to design high-end scaffolds for pre-surgical training and planning. Propelled by high growth prospects, VisionAir Solutions, a US-based company launched 3D-printed silicone stents. Manufactured by integrating 3D printing and medical-grade silicone injection, the novel system is specifically designed for patient’s suffering from central airway obstructions. The company’s cloud-based platform is allowing pulmonologists to design customized silicone stents. Spectroplast, a Switzerland-based start-up, developed silicon additive manufacturing (SAM)-based 3D printer. The advanced technology is assisting manufacturers in designing silicon-based orthotics, implants, and various other anatomical devices. Driven by its abilities to offer cost-efficient and customized solutions, the demand for SAM 3D printers is witnessing a significant increase.
  • Internet of Medical Things - The introduction of Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) devices is allowing clinicians to easily access, transmit, and analyse a wide array of medical data. Meanwhile, the advanced system is assisting patients in easily sharing their medical history with hospitals. Manufactured by using state of the art technology, IoMT is allowing medical professionals to constantly monitor patient movements. Driven by its ability to offer cost-efficient and reliable solutions, the demand for IoMT systems is gaining momentum. This is propelling various globally recognized manufacturers to devise strategies for entering the highly prospective segment. For instance, Orbicor, a Costa-Rica based firm launched UnnoMed, a cloud-based cardiovascular management platform. The advanced technology utilizes IoMT systems for generating a wide range of critical healthcare data. The novel system allows medical professionals to easily diagnose various cardiovascular ailments at an earlier stage. Fuelled by high growth prospects, EloCare, a Singapore-based start-up developed Elo, a connected wearable menopause care system. The compact device allows users to constantly collect and monitor data pertaining to various health parameters. This is assisting medical professionals in developing customized treatment plan for women suffering from menopause disease.
  • Minimally Invasive Devices -A majority of patients refrain from undergoing surgical procedures, due to fear of contracting infections, slow recovery, and large scars. However, the advent of minimally invasive devices is enabling physicians to safely perform laparoscopy, endoscopy, and various other surgical procedures. Further, propelled by their ability to minimize trauma and curtail healthcare costs, the demand for minimally invasive devices is gaining momentum. In order to tap the available opportunity, ReGelTec, a US-based start-up developed Hydrafil, an injectable hydrogel equipment. The compact device utilizes hydrogel technology to inject an implant for augmenting presence of viscous fluid and nucleus pulposus. The advanced system is currently being used by physicians to manufacture implants for patients having severe back pain. Additionally, Synovetin OA manufactured by Exubrion Therapeutics is currently being used by veterinary doctors for treating patients suffering from synovitis. One of the major advantages associated with using Synovetin OA is that it produces no side effects. As a result, the novel technology is gaining acceptance among various medical professionals for providing targeted therapy to osteoarthritis patients.
  • Medical Waste Management - Constantly increasing medical waste is propelling companies to undertake appropriate procedures for ensuring their safe disposal. In order to address this concern, manufacturers are giving a lot of impetus on using recyclable materials for devising biomedical applications. Driven by promising outlook, FLEEP Technologies is now offering recyclable biopatches under its product range. The company utilizes carbon-based inks and plastic films for developing recyclable electronic patches. Similarly, Polycarbin, a US-based company developed a medical waste management platform. The platform is specifically designed for enabling laboratories to easily recycle a wide range of plastic waste. Apart from this, the firm also developed a mobile application that allow users to constantly monitor recycling process.

  • 5G Technology - Owing to its significantly higher bandwidth, 5G technology significantly minimizes risks pertaining to data latency. Driven by its ability to ensure high-speed communication among various connected devices, the demand for 5G is witnessing a robust increase. Increasing application of 5G in healthcare sector is allowing medical professionals to easily convert images on a real-time basis. This is assisting them in easily diagnosis diseases at an earlier stage, thereby ensuring timely treatment for patients. Propelled by high growth prospects, Spline, an India-based company introduced Dr. Spline. The integrated healthcare platform utilizes both AI and 5G technologies for offering easy to use, safe, and reliable solution for end users. Similarly, M2JN, a UK-based start-up developed a 5G-enabled medical patch for monitoring various cardiovascular symptoms. The innovative system is specifically designed for enabling medical practitioners to easily diagnose various cardiovascular ailments. Apart from this, the advanced technology is also assisting clinicians in monitoring healing of post-surgical and diabetic wounds.
  • Cybersecurity - Despite various advantages associated with their use, connected and cloud-based medical devices remains vulnerable to cyber-attacks. Propelled by growing data security concerns, healthcare service providers are now integrating high-end cyber security mechanisms. The novel technologies are enabling both hospitals and patients to easily identify any network as well as device anomalies. As a result, demand for advanced cyber security solutions is gaining acceptance among various healthcare practitioners. For instance, Culinda is offering end to end medical equipment security management solutions under its product range. The advanced systems utilize AI to protect medical devices from a wide range of cyber-attacks. In addition, the novel technology uses blockchain for enabling users to constantly access a broad range of confidential information. Scope OmniSight designed by Scope Security utilizes AI to prevent data theft from electronic health record, IT systems, and various other medical devices. Owing to their superior features, use of cyber security software in healthcare sector is expected to witness a robust surge.
  • Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality Enabled Devices – The advent of virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and extended reality (XR) technologies are allowing customers to access high-resolution videos as well as images. Introduction of 3D enabled AR and VR devices are enabling healthcare professionals to streamline workflow. In addition, the advanced systems are assisting medical practitioners in ensuring timely treatment for patients. Owing to their lightweight and portable framework, the novel systems are allowing doctors to examine patients even at their homes. This is playing an important role in offering high-end healthcare services for elderly, underprivileged, as well as patients with cognitive impairment. Driven by its ability to treat Alzheimer’s disease and safely perform surgical procedures, demand for VR, AR, and XR-enabled medical devices is gaining momentum. Fuelled by high growth prospects, companies are now manufacturing medical equipment with integrated digital visualization systems. In order to gain competitive advantage, manufacturers are entering into collaboration with optical design companies. The novel strategy is assisting them in developing high-end coatings and lenses for VR and AR-enabled medical devices. Despite promising outlook, there continues to remain certain challenges that are impeding demand for the novel technologies. For instance, various medical professionals complain of neck pain, fatigue, and dizziness after using AR and VR devices. In order to address this concern, manufacturers are focusing their attention towards developing lightweight easy to use AR and VR-enabled medical equipment.
  • Artificial Intelligence in Medical Devices - Increasing application of AI technology is enabling medical practitioners to access as well as analyse a wide range of critical healthcare data. This is assisting clinicians in easily diagnosing various diseases, along with safely performing surgical procedures. Propelled by its growing acceptance, various leading global manufacturers are now offering AI-enabled medical devices for end users. However, in order to ensure patient safety, Food and Drug Administration (FDA), Medical Device Regulation (MDR), and In Vitro Medical Device Regulation (IVDR) introduced various stringent regulations and guidelines. Apart from this, it is also important for manufacturers to constantly evaluate performance of their AI-enabled devices. Constantly increasing popularity of the advanced equipment is propelling various leading global firms to make a foray into the highly prospective segment. Going by the trend, Nightingale introduced HEARThermo, a AI-enabled wearable device. The advanced technology allows users to constantly monitor heart beat rate as well as blood pressure levels. Manufactured by using state of the art technology, the high-end equipment allows healthcare workers predict an infection outbreak. Similarly, PneumoWave also introduced AI-enabled chest-worn smart biosensor under its product portfolio for healthcare solutions. The novel technology utilizes ML algorithms for enabling medical practitioners to monitor patients suffering from chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Driven by their abilities to improve healthcare, the global market for AI-enabled medical devices is expected witness a robust growth.
  • Metaverse – Metaverse incorporates AI, VR, and AR technologies, thereby enabling medical practitioners to gain comprehensive understanding regarding diseases. In order to curtail spread of dangerous corona virus, governments introduced stringent social distancing guidelines. This propelled healthcare practitioners to use automated and digital technologies at their workplaces. Introduction of block chain, telemedicine, and digital technologies are allowing medical professionals to provide timely treatment for patients. Driven by their abilities to enhance operational efficiency, metaverse is gaining acceptance among biotechnology and pharmaceutical sectors. The advanced technology is currently being used for providing training to clinicians as well as medical students. Propelled by its ability to provide affordable and inclusive treatment, various leading global technological companies are making a foray into the metaverse space. Going by the trend, LG Electronics voiced intentions to develop high-end Metaverse, electric mobility, and digital health device manufacturing facilities. In order to fulfil this requirement, the company enters into collaboration with nine startups such as NeuroTrainer, XRHealth, Mindset Medical, LifeNome, and Digbi Health. Similarly, XRHealth, a leading global manufacturer of VR solutions also launched NeuroReality under its therapeutic VR product range. The advanced technology is specifically designed for treating patients suffering from various brain injuries and strokes. Novarad Corporation (US), Siemens Healthineers (Germany), Brainlab AG (Germany), and GE Healthcare (US) are some of the leading companies in the global metaverse market.
