WHAT'S TRENDING

Internet of Things (IoT) is redefining connectivity. IoT is driving change in the 21st century at dizzying speeds. IoT has been growing enormously thanks to the development of three main technologies that include artificial intelligence, big data, and cloud computing. Increasing adoption of the IoT is transforming the way organizations operate as well as share and generate end-point data. Businesses are able to store huge amounts of data that are created by IoT at economical rates because of the easy availability of affordable storage on the cloud. Big data technologies help in managing and understanding the data that is created by IoT systems. Artificial intelligence, as well as its related technologies, have allowed to a generation of information that aids businesses to grow.

The number of connected IoT devices is expected to increase substantially in the coming years. Companies are anticipated to continue embracing IoT to achieve automation and offer more intelligence. In addition, organizations adopting IoT are likely to focus on efforts to make the technology faster and smarter. Mobile devices like smartphones, tablets and other hand held devices and IoT devices that communicate with other machines are witnessing the highest growth. The proliferation of IoT technology is almost universal in virtually every sector of society, economy and life. Connected devices are today increasingly becoming ubiquitous in manufacturing plants, warehouses, business processes, logistics systems, retail stores, hospitals, public/government services, smart cities, and consumer homes, among others. Individuals and organizations, by using sophisticated computing systems and algorithms to capture, store and analyze data, could ensure selective storage and sharing of information, as well as enhance the analytical powers.

In the emerging world with more machines than people, IoT is poised to takes the center stage. As the fastest growing man made phenomenon, the rise of the machines is expanding the addressable market opportunity for IoT. The growth of machines especially mobile devices is continuing spiral at rates 5 times faster than population growth. Mobile devices like smartphones, tablets and other hand held devices and IoT devices that communicate with other machines are witnessing the highest growth. Other machines include smart meters, cars, industrial equipment etc. Today, over a large portion of IoT activity is centered in manufacturing, transportation/logistics, smart city/utility and consumer applications. Over the next two years demand response, distributed energy generation and storage, smart meters, and fleet management will emerge as largest IoT spending categories. Applications with medium-term potential include automated inventory management, and predictive equipment maintenance.

IoT technology is expected to boom in the near future. The rise of 5G is likely to impact the IoT market, as 5G technology can provide high-speed transmission capabilities to not only present but also new IoT networks. Growing security issues are likely to compel businesses to implement data security as well as privacy approach starting from the designing phase and implement it at various levels via the IoT architecture layers. Moving forward, as IoT systems generate huge amounts of data, product manufacturing companies are likely to move towards adopting a service ecosystem.

The number of connected devices being installed is anticipated to grow tremendously in the years to come, indicating a very promising, revolutionary, and innovative future for the IoT sector. Some of the trends that are expected to transform the face of IoT include: -


  • Single Interface - Today, a growing number of devices with varied capabilities are becoming part of the IoT network. Users, enterprises, or individuals would require a single interface that can enable them to access end-to-end instead of an array of interfaces and remotes.
  • Shift from Products to Services Ecosystem- Key factor driving any IoT system is the data that is created by the system. Businesses offering products are likely to move to services around the understanding given by the data.
  • Multi-Level Privacy and Security - Data security as well as privacy is very crucial. Data security, historically, has been made available at a device level such as a laptop or smartphone, or any other kind of smart device that is connected to the IoT network. Nonetheless, in the coming years, IoT is expected to be provided at various layers between IoT endpoints.
  • 5G Integration - Many companies started testing the 5G network in 2020. IoT architecture is expected to be integrated with 5G into the old as well as new systems. 5G has the potential to improve the capabilities of present network manifolds. 5G would not supersede the current network but improve its capabilities. 5G network would be able to transmit data to and from cloud servers at great speed.
  • Regulatory Landscape - Regulation is glared by technology enthusiasts and developers, however, IoT is a technology that is capable of affecting institutions and people in a way that is generally not related to any other kind of technology. This is mainly due to data, which is the major driver of any IoT system, and data is highly prone to be misused. Thus, in order to protect the data governments across the world have been issuing rules and regulations for the adoption of IoT systems.

  • Remote Monitoring - This is a type of software that enables Managed Service Providers (MSPs) to manage and if required supervise the IT systems, enabling various necessary interferences. For instance, installing the software remotely to devices or watching the behavior of a managed device for diagnostics and analyzing the performance. IoT’s remote monitoring feature has been used for observing occupancy levels and structural integrity in the buildings, and performance of the machine, and automated learning, resulting in its foreseen application as one of the key impacts of IoT in the upcoming years.
  • Healthcare - The outbreak of the recent COVID-19 pandemic led to the rise of various new solutions in the healthcare industry, and this is expected to be the top trend for IoT in 2023 and beyond. IoT has infiltrated from business-level development to patient realities and consumer realities, in the form of specialized smart devices for the medical field. A significant milestone among many is the development of in-home sensors and wearable devices which enable healthcare professionals and doctors to help during an emergency. These devices allow healthcare professionals and doctors to monitor their patients after they have been discharged. This signifies further advances in telemedicine and faster access during emergencies, among other benefits. Some of the specialized smart devices for use in the medical field include pressure gauges, smart scales, watches, and fitness bracelets to name a few. One of the biggest advantages of these devices is that they are capable of analyzing and transferring correct measurements of an individual’s basic indicators, enabling doctors to tailor the treatment accordingly. Further, the devices also enable medical professionals to examine the side effects or efficacy of the administered treatment, minimizing the gap between medicine and response to the medicine. Areas that display immense opportunities for the growth of IoT systems in the medical sector include asset management that enables pharmacists to track for instance humidity levels and temperature. Another popular growth area for IoT systems includes hygiene monitoring devices that are capable of minimizing the risks of patient infection.
  • Data Analysis - Data is one of the most crucial strategic weapons for businesses as it enables all stakeholders to plan and implement their strategies while making appropriate forecasts about the future. With purchasing and selling of IoT data expected to become very crucial in developing IoT, it is likely that the IoT displays immense potential in data collection as well as analysis in various ways. A classic example of this can be the usage of IoT devices in the supply chain segment. Businesses can minimize the impact and rule out any kind of crisis and ensure the efficient working of supply chains by enabling supply chain elements to gather data from interaction points and transfer it at a faster rate. Data generated by IoT systems can also be utilized through marketing in a more customer-based way. Businesses can supervise the behavior of their customers through household IoT devices such as smart meters or virtual assistants, and enable the businesses to customize their marketing strategies in line with their customers’ requirements.
  • Information Exchange - All businesses realize how invaluable data is. Infonomics is the collection, analysis, and trade of data like other commodities, and is poised to gain prominence in 2023 with its upward growth trajectory. Sectors such as agriculture, hospitality, retail, and healthcare will witness the impact of this growing trend.
  • AI and IoT - IoT and AI have a lot in common so integrating AI with IoT can increase efficiency, improve human-machine communication, and facilitate robust data analysis and collection, among others. Cross-pollinating the two future-forward technologies will dramatically increase, enrich, and improve the digital experience. AIoT devices are likely to increase drastically in the years ahead.
  • Smart Cities - Smart cities have been developed in the past few years for incorporating all the IoT data collection methods. The concept focuses on offering a citizen-friendly, financially feasible, and ecological way of managing the metropolitan area. Smart cities make use of initiatives, including meters, lights, and connected sensors for gathering and analyzing data, which is subsequently executed to adapt and enhance utilities and infrastructure. An innovative aspect of smart city developments includes the various innovations surrounding virtual representations of physical items. For instance, Amsterdam has embraced an integrated smart city model and launched an incentivization system, wherein citizens are given energy storage units whenever they connect their houses to the smart grid. This enables constant monitoring of the voltage and current of the electricity, thus leading to a reduction of energy costs and development in the performance of CO2.
  • Integration of IoT and Cloud Computing - Integration links two distinct technologies to fulfill a specific goal. The IoT cloud serves as a centralized server which offers access to computer resources at any given time. With cloud computing, large data packets generated by the IoT can conveniently transit. In its basic form, cloud integration entails the combination of many cloud environments as several public clouds or a hybrid deployment to enable them to work as one unified IT infrastructure in a company. In IoT, a cloud computing system stores this data in a shared environment with access to centralized computer resources on the server that is possible whenever required. IoT-driven Internet data packets move easily across cloud computing. IoT removes the reliance on on-premises infrastructure because of its massive storage capacity and has entered the mainstream owing to constant developments in Internet-based technology like the devices and Internet that support innovative cloud solutions. With the automation of tasks through IoT and cloud technology, organizations can significantly lower security threats. In addition, it offers robust security measures by protecting users with effective encryption and authentication protocols. Contemporary technology seamlessly integrates cloud and IoT while also enabling real-time connectivity and communication. This facilitates real-time data collection, real-time data integration, non-stop connectivity, and on-spot analysis for major business processes. A cloud service utilizes a data server network that is spread across several geographical locations which store many copies of back-up data. Cloud computing offers the easy recovery of data from various IoT-based operations in an emergency. As a key element of IoT, cloud computing acts as the foundation for data administration and storage, analytics and security, among others. Since cloud computing relies on countless devices connecting to the Internet, cloud will play a critical role in helping businesses unleash the potential of ever-increasing IoT data. Lastly, IoT offers tremendous potential but only if businesses can embrace innovation and leverage the right technology.
  • Security Emerges as a Big Challenge for IoT - Security and IoT have a very complex relationship. On one hand, IoT devices are very helpful and make routine work easier, however, these devices also pose a high risk of cyberattacks. As the number of IoT devices increases the risk of cyberattacks also increases. Most of the users lack proper education about IoT devices, besides there is a high probability that the data can be stolen or misplaced and give access to unauthorized users. The network of hijacked devices is often managed by illegal cyber personnel and is used for launching distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks that distort the target’s service, server, or network by transmitting an enormous flow of internet traffic. DDoS attacks make use of various insinuated systems as sources for attack traffic that can consist of computers and IoT devices. This poses an increased threat as administrators cannot control the devices that get connected to the network. As vendors do not consider this a major threat, there has not been any regulatory guidance to safeguard the customers. Hence, this could pose an even greater challenge while integrating IoT with businesses. Though it is extremely convenient to have multiple devices connected online, it also raises the risk of hacking and cyberattacks. To address this, 2023 will witness more robust security measures that decrease the chances of exposing digital data to any menace. Tighter security for various IoT devices will be critical in preventing hacking and cyberattacks. The US National Security Council will launch its security labeling system for standardization in early 2023.
