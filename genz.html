WHAT'S TRENDING

Driven by constantly increasing population, number of people within Gen Z age group is expected to account for 27% of the global workforce through 2025. In order to tap the opportunity, several globally recognized companies are now devising a wide range of novel strategies. Under this initiative, they are taking into consideration constantly evolving Gen Z trends. Businesses, policymakers, and marketers are increasingly shifting their focus from millennials to Gen Z, who are beginning to enter workplaces. Gen Z individuals, also referred to as Centennials and Generation Edge, are often more self-assured and less self-absorbed. These individuals also possess a resourceful, resilient, and realistic nature. Despite these differing opinions, Gen Z is expected to be the consumers of the future. Thus, businesses that intend to gain a competitive edge are required to build trust with this generation by understanding such individuals, their environment, and the way their experiences can shape their future lives.


The oldest individuals of Gen Z have barely completed their graduation, while many others are still in their education life. Thus, the spending patterns of Gen Z are expected to fluctuate significantly in the upcoming years as these individuals become more confident and independent in their decisions. The environment in which Gen Z individuals are growing up can be analyzed to determine its impact on their future. The intention of Gen Z to bring positive difference, which represents the distinction between purpose and cause, is a crucial aspect for marketers and business owners. Gen Z is anticipated to be defined by purpose in place of cause, which defined the millennials. Thus, organizations and brands are required to become enablers of their purpose by realizing a balance between their wider responsibility and business objectives.


Gen Z primarily possesses paradoxical views as these individuals have grown up in a connected world where technology has proliferated in all aspects of human lives. Although these individuals are realists who understand the effect of environmental, social, and economic issues, they are optimists with a robust work ethic that value their contributions to improving the global community. Fluid communication forms, global citizenship, and technological literacy are responsible for these traits in Gen Z. Thus, brands have to identify ways that can play a role in saving the world to connect with Gen Z individuals possessing altruistic and unique personality traits. In contrast to their millennial predecessors, Gen Zers have been familiarized with the failures of corporate social responsibility (CSR). The Internet and activism have created a society where any amount of paid advertising cannot prevent the exposure of paper-thin intentions.

Businesses have to change both their structure and attitude by shifting the function of social responsibility from the marketing department and applying it to their entire business operations. Brands that have aligned their visions with the intentions of Gen Z are witnessing significant benefits. For instance, Nationwide connected with Gen Z customers through empowerment and authenticity. Brands are required to adapt to the preferences of every new generation to survive and flourish. For instance, branded content witnessed substantial growth when millennials became the dominant group of consumers. Similarly, the most popular businesses that resonate with Gen Z and Gen Y have been built to facilitate personal and authentic interaction in place of selling products. Brands that intend to thrive in the upcoming decades have to adopt this approach of human connection and authenticity through app-based models or other creative ways. Brands are required to adopt new methods to research consumers as Gen Z consumers demand more relevance and authenticity from brands. Brands can integrate moment-to-moment interactions with big data analysis and obtain an in-depth qualitative understanding to effectively align themselves with the interests of the new, world-changing, exciting generation of consumers.


Generational shifts are anticipated to play a more crucial role in influencing behavior with the increasing global connectivity compared to socioeconomic differences. Young individuals have become influential on individuals of all incomes and ages and on the way individuals in other age groups relate to and consume brands. The current generation of influencers, primarily the Gen Z members are considered true digital natives as they have experienced mobile systems, the Internet, and social networks from their earliest youth. Thus, the Gen Z generation is considered a hypercognitive generation that is extremely comfortable with cross-referencing and collecting several information sources and integrating offline and virtual experiences. Gen Zers avoid labels and give significance to individual expressions. These individuals mobilize themselves for different causes that are good for society and believe in dialogue to resolve conflicts for a better world. These behaviors influence the consumption patterns of Gen Zers and their relationships with brands. Thus, companies have to align their approach with Gen Z by considering consumption as a matter of ethical concern, an expression of personal identity, and as access in place of possession. The generation shift, coupled with advancements in technology, has fundamentally altered the consumer landscape in every socioeconomic bracket by permeating the entire demographic pyramid, extending beyond Gen Z.

The concept of manifesting the personal identity is the core of Gen Zers, which makes consumption a way of self-expression in place of adhering to the norms of certain groups. Thus, consumers of all generations are becoming more interested in personalized products and willing to spend more on such products that display their individuality following the footsteps of millennials and Gen Z. For instance, a considerable share of both A-class and C-class consumers are ready to pay more for personalized product offerings, and a substantial share of such consumers has expressed no hesitation to spend more products from brands that align with the consumer’s causes and interests. A significant share of Gen Zers increasingly prefers brands that avoid gender-based classification of items. Despite the expectations concerning personalization, consumers in all generations are not completely comfortable about personal data sharing with companies, with a substantial share of consumers having issues concerning personal data sharing, even in case there is complete transparency in the process.


Consumers are expected to consume services and products at any place and time due to the convergence of offline and online worlds, which has increased the significance of improving omnichannel sales and marketing. Companies can utilize advanced analytics to improve their consumer data insights as customer information long stored in data repositories currently has a strategic value. In certain instances, the information is enough to create value. Thus, leading companies have to implement a data strategy that can assist them in developing business insights by interpreting and collecting information about individual consumers while ensuring data privacy. Retailers and consumer companies have to adopt a two-track model, including a model for mass consumption and scale and another model for customization to cater to the most loyal consumers or specific consumer groups. In such a scenario, manufacturing and supply chain processes, along with marketing, have to become more flexible and agile.


Consumption-based on ethics


Eventually, consumers expect companies and brands to “take a stand”, specifically to select a particular cause or topic that makes sense for them and their consumers and can clearly articulate or communicate about the selected issue. Younger consumers do not differentiate between brand ethics, the company owning that brand, and the network of suppliers and partners of the company in a transparent environment. Thus, the actions of a company are required to match the company ideals, and the ideals have to permeate throughout the stakeholder system. Gen Zers are well-informed about brands and their realities as they can quickly access information and develop a perspective about them. Thus, these consumers can easily notice the contradictions between the actions and communications of a brand.


A substantial share of consumers prefers to purchase from companies that are considered ethical by them and refuse to purchase from companies involved in scandals and controversies. Almost two-thirds of consumers strive to know about the origins of the product they intend to purchase, specifically the ingredients of the product, the way the product has been made, and the location of its manufacturing. More than half of the consumers consider recommendations from friends a reliable source to learn about brands and products. Most consumers, specifically Gen Zers, are tolerant of mistakes by brands in case the brands rectify the mistakes.


A significant share of consumers considers small brands more ethical than major brands, which can be a challenging prospect for large companies. Work ethics and marketing are converging from the consumer perspective, which made clear identification of topics by organizations for which they are expected to take a stand and ensuring every stakeholder in the entire value chain perfectly aligns with the identified causes essential. Thus, companies have to carefully select marketing agents who represent their products and brands. Consumers often tend to follow influencers, such as Instagram personas with 5000-20000 followers, as they increasingly understand the strategy of brands to subsidize their influencers. In the digital age, marketing poses complex challenges as channels are becoming more ever-changing and fragmented.

Larger number of GenZ now opt for plant based diets led by ethical concerns and health benefits. Additionally, they are inclined towards trying different Asian cuisines including Indian, Korean, and Japanese. Online Reviews Play a Clear Role in Making a Choice: In contrast to their predecessors, Gen Z generation are relatively more accustomed in using latest gadgets as well as digital technologies. Gen Z has lived through tumultuous times Having experienced various stressors, such as political unrest, climate change, school shootings, and pandemic.
