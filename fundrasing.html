WHAT'S TRENDING

Whether online or offline, raising funds for a cause has never been easy. It is an art that continues to be perfected even today in all its forms, be it capital campaigns, corporate support, online fundraising, earned income, grants, membership campaigns or special events. There is growing awareness among NGO and not-for-profit organizations about the overhyped effectiveness of social media. Despite all that has been said about social media, many nonprofits have realized that the return on investment (ROI) from using social media has been but negligible. There is a distinct shift therefore now to focus resources on the neglected digital marketing and fundraising tools. As disengagement from social media platforms gains momentum, a parallel focus will be shed on website redesign, email marketing for fundraising, designing and implementing a donor retention strategy, investments in innovative graphics design i.e. social media posts & banners; Infographics; email templates; website banners etc. Also expected to make a comeback and rise in popularity are tribute giving programs; adoption of Instagram fundraising tools; advertise on streaming platforms like Twitch.tv.


Streaming platforms deserve special attention as young and wealthy media consumers including gamers cannot be reached through traditional outreach programs as they have a very low probability of responding to email pitches, gifts, or DMs. Building robust relationships with streamers can unlock a magic source of resources. NGOs will also be seen increasingly focusing on updating their knowledge on online privacy laws and prioritizing cybersecurity strategies. These strategies will include using premium hosting service for their websites, investing in tools that prioritize speed, security, and automated back-ups, deploying double opt-in and captcha for email subscriptions, and implementing two-factor authentication and other password management tools.


Crowdfunding is emerging as another efficient way of bringing in money for projects but it is different from fundraising. While fundraising implies seeking financial aid/support for charity and such other related causes, crowdfunding constitutes the practice followed by budding entrepreneurs and businesses accumulating money from individuals for funding their business ideas.

  • Fundraising in the Metaverse - Currently, several charities are capitalizing on the metaverse fundraising opportunities. These charities are the ones that exhibit higher risk tolerance and have greater access to tech expertise. They could both be the small organizations with volunteers or leadership that are tech-savvy and mega-charity organizations with the capability of hiring highly able development teams. One of the key advantages with metaverse fundraising is that it can bring together people and communities that care about issues. The internet facilitates this metaverse fundraising. Often, due to dearth of resources and funding initially, non-profits cannot afford launching massive promotional campaigns for attracting considerable attention to their cause. An increasing number of such charitable organizations therefore are starting to recognize advantages that advanced technologies can offer. AR/VR and such other advanced technologies for instance, can offer substantial benefits to non-profits. Sometimes, a simple story or a photo exhibition would not be enough for raising the needed awareness for achieving a fundraising goal. The problem could be conveyed more efficiently and faster with VR and AR technologies which provide a more interactive and engaging solution. Such campaigns stand a better chance of raising needed funds for a cause importantly, in a shorter time duration.
  • AR Apps Increase Effectiveness of Fundraising for Charities & Nonprofits - AR apps are increasing effectiveness of fundraising campaigns for non-profits and charities. The apps are increasing awareness levels for the projects and engaging more people on deeper emotional levels to empathize and donate to causes. Using AR apps can also be a cost-effective method as the non-profits would not be required to invest much in developing and deploying the apps. However, organizations intending to create a highly effective AR app for charity would be required to analyze meticulously as to what interaction type could be ideal for their cause. AR apps help non-profits in building awareness about their causes, in an effective manner. More people would come to know of the problem which would make resolving it easier. The apps allow charities to involve more people by covering more ground. When more people become aware then more donations can be expected. The apps also help charities show the problem at hand form a completely new angle which helps in getting potential givers deeper into issues. They could have a ‘Donate’ button for calling people to act instantaneously. Charities can also use AR apps for providing potential givers with updated information as the app processing is conducted in the real-time. Non-profits could push updates on-the-go ensuring that users have access to the latest data at all times.
  • Virtual Reality in Charity -Charities today are seeking newer ways of getting across their messages to potential philanthropy givers. Merely making a problem known is not adequate currently as for making donors empathise with an issue they are required to be shown, and only then they would be able to better connect with the depths of issues/crisis for which the donations are being sought. Charity organizations therefore are actively exploring the technology of VR that helps them place viewers inside the issue/crisis, literally, thus enabling them to achieve the maximum empathy as well as the intention to help. VR makes such effects possible as it can create an immersive experience. Viewers can be transported to virtual environments of distant physical places. VR can recreate disastrous situations for helping viewers focus their attention. VR is also being used by NPOs to increase awareness about issues in addition to raising funds. NPOs and charities also use VR for reporting on how the funds have been spent. The organizations can create film documentaries, photos and report the actions taken with the money accumulated. When givers are able to see how their donated money is spent, campaigns would have a stronger impact.
  • Blockchain for Charity Fundraising: Behind the Buzzword - With well-established digital currencies charities are now able to engage with newer category of donors. Fundraising platforms are able to accept cryptocurrencies, which offer charities several benefits. Charities can track & trace donations bringing in complete transparency. Payments from across the world can be accepted, eliminating foreign exchange fee. It enables trading in non-geographic currency which would be a major benefit for charities located in some of the world’s difficult parts. Already, several major charities started accepting these currencies. Major names like Salvation Army and UNICEF have registered on platforms like cryptogivingtuesday.org for accepting cryptos like Ethereum and Bitcoin among others. Charities including the Royal National Lifeboat Institution and Breast Cancer Support in the UK are also accepting digital currencies. The verifiable transactions trail of Blockchain makes funds usage highly transparent. Donors would be able to track what they have donated. There are also platforms now that can use Blockchain for mining the unused computing capacity of donors and convert it into money for charities. An example is the Give Byte platform. There is also much research underway for developing new cryptocurrency applications for charities. BitGive for instance, collaborated with the University of Edinburgh, for development of such applications. All these indicate the growing interest worldwide in developing blockchain use cases for charities. Nonetheless, the concept of cryptocurrencies for charities has its own drawbacks foremost among which is the lack of awareness around the technology. The RNLI was the first charity to adopt the digital currency, way back in the year 2014, and admitted to facing several obstacles in accepting donations because of their price volatilities. Such price volatility makes it hard for charities to create and execute any plans around the currencies’ value. However, the volatility at the same time can work for the benefit of both charities and the givers. Currency appreciation for instance, can allow donors to donate the purchased cryptocurrencies at lower prices, leaving the charities with the appreciating price advantage. Cryptocurrencies and blockchain may be mysterious to most people even now but the interest in them is growing. In the years ahead, more individual charities would also come to accept them.
  • Digital Marketing for Nonprofit Fundraisers - Digital fundraising implies building an online community for raising money. It follows pretty much the same rules as the offline fundraising but there are no appeal or project style approaches. Similar to how digital marketing works, digital fundraising is all about attracting the target person, with the most appropriate message, at the most opportune time. Digital fundraising represents targeting specific people using specific messages for ultimately personalizing for them, the overall experience of donation for a cause. It is basically the cycle of attracting and nurturing people and finally converting them into donors. It is fluid and continuous and not segmented as projects. Digital fundraising comes in different forms – peer-2-peer fundraising, social sharing of messages and campaigns, online crowdfunding, special events, and interactive online among others. It is in many ways similar to digital marketing in that it involves telling stories, leveraging and engaging peer networks with community, building relations and transmitting to them right messages at the right time. SEO is the basic concept in digital marketing for organizations. It makes their websites rank high in the searches for words or phrases connecting to that organization. For charities, a similar SEO strategy can translate into more donations. It can get more visitors to their website. NPOs must therefore brainstorm their key phrases and implement SEO practices for improving results. Another strategy is to use email newsletter. This is an important way of bringing potential donors into the subsequent stage in digital fundraising, that of nurturing. Personalized emails can be more effective in acquiring donors. NPOs can reach potential donors for telling them about their organization through emails. An email newsletter can be sent at regular intervals for building the audience. Graphic updates can be provided for making known to the receiver about all that is happening in the NPO including the ongoing campaigns. Receivers must also be able to easily subscribe. Prompts or pop-ups can be used in key places throughout the newsletter helping them subscribe.

    Non-profit fundraisers can also effectively use social media sites. Seeing names of organizations on the sites would encourage givers to become part of such communities. However, fundraisers must first decide presence in which sites would make more sense for their organization. For Instagram for instance, good graphics and images would be necessary. NPOs must also keep their social media accounts up-to-date and active always. They must be able to respond immediately to comments. Mixing content can also be an effective strategy. Some of the content can be for promoting services and programs of the organization while the other content can be for seeking donations. There must always be a good amount of content that offers something for free, for followers. It can be in the form of educational information, interesting or fun content about the organization, ways to become connected with the community or even tricks and tips. Social media tales evoke the necessary emotion in people about causes and once the donors have become engaged emotionally, they must be told the subsequent steps reaching up to the actual donation. Fundraisers must also ensure that their social media pages could be found easily on their websites and in their newsletters. Social media ads can also be used. Having business pages on social media sites can be helpful. Non-profit fundraisers are also required to well manage their data using the best data management systems. The data they capture can help streamline their efforts of fundraising. Fundraisers would be able to create engagement opportunities that would be donor-specific. Information like the occupation, average gift amount and frequency of donating among others can help fundraisers determine aspects such as how to ask and how much to expect. Data also enables fundraisers to segment donors and prioritize fundraising efforts.

    Shifting to social fundraising would also be essential for non-profits going forward. Advertising was compelled to evolve more rapidly in the recent years with the average span of human attention dropping to eight seconds, an all-time low. Fundraising efforts are expected to evolve similarly for building audience. The number of clicks reaching up to the donation must reduce. They can be no more than five clicks in order to sustain user interest. People today intend to make donations where they are, which is in the social medial networks. Too arduous a donation process or the one requiring way too many clicks, fundraisers would risk losing donor interest. In addition to minimizing donor journey, the social network content in the surrounding also offers fundraisers the opportunities of educating and influencing people while they are trying to make a donation. For all these benefits therefore, non-profits are required to shift to digital-first, social fundraising. However, non-profits generally resist this shift because it compels them to let go of some amount of control they so far had. They would now be at the mercy of the ever-changing algorithms of social networks. Non-profits therefore are required to become comfortable with social fundraising first if they are to see more money for their charity projects. Furthermore, social media cannot just be used for building online communities but also to capture recurring donations. It helps in keeping participants engaged through hosting repeat events for fundraising. It can thus help create more predictable and also consistent revenue streams. Using capabilities of messaging automation in social media chats can also be impactful in personalizing interactions. Facebook Messenger and such others can be especially valuable, with their higher open rates. Recipients typically enjoy the quick intakes and short content bursts provided. However, notwithstanding the vehicle selected, the messaging must be highly personalized. Non-profits can thus leverage social media and its inherent powers to the fullest for enabling friction free giving, repeatability of giving, and also personalization of messages.
  • Fundraisers to Target Gen-Zers & Millennials - Going forward, fundraisers would target Gen-Zers in addition to millennials for donations. These next-generation givers would change the landscape of funds giving for charity and other related purposes. Both generations grew up being told by adults about how they could make the world a better place to live by giving. Now adults, this population would show that belief in action. However, these people belonging to these generations have high regard for their time. Time for them is money. Non-profit fundraisers therefore must understand the differences between the inspirations for baby boomers and the millennials and Gen Zers and adopt the most appropriate donor engagement strategies. The expectations of these new generations are different and fundraisers are required to offer them multiple ways for becoming engaged. They must be given payment options that include credit and debit cards and other online options. The website must be eye-catching and mobile friendly. Younger donors must be given the opportunity to share the fundraiser’s mission with family and friends. This would expand the donor base for the non-profit. They must also be given volunteering opportunities which helps build a longer-lasting relationship of an organization with a donor.
  • Leveraging Data - Non-profits would also leverage the increasing amount of data for philanthropy. They would use ML and predictive analytics for measuring the extent and impact of their reach. Technologies would also be used for personalizing giving experience for donors. Donors’ communication preferences and interests would be taken into consideration and personalized messages created for developing stronger relationships with the organizations.
  • Privacy of Donors Becomes Key - Donor privacy would also be given due significance in the years ahead. In 2021, the Mail Privacy Protection Policy was launched by Apple. Prior to this policy, it was possible for non-profits to collect engagement data pertaining to their email campaigns through inserting few hidden pixels in the body of the email so that when they are opened, the pixels would be loaded allowing the host of the mail to see that it was opened. It also revealed the type of devices used for opening the email and the IP address of the recipient. The IP address offered the estimated location of the recipient, up to the level of the city. The new policy of privacy protection however, prevented email marketers from tracking data points. Non-profits therefore are required to seek alternative metrics for assessing their email campaign results and they are trying to use click rate, unsubscribe rate and conversion rate among others, which provide them with a broader overview of their campaigns’ performance. Such policies focused on increasing donor privacy are compelling non-profits to seek alternative measurement methods. Non-profits have also been adapting to the practice of cleaning out the unsubscribed and bounced email addresses, which leaves them with the most engaged audience.
  • Flexible Giving & Participation Gains Importance - Another trend that would be huge over the coming years would be that of flexible participation and giving. The pandemic brought forth many changes to the ways people interacted with businesses, especially non-profits. Donors are currently expecting organizations to provide them options for participating from the comfort and safety of their homes. Several donors have over the past two years moved online and the trend would continue. Also, when flexibility is offered, donors could be enticed to donate more. Over the past two years, the way events for fundraising were held also changed. Events were held mostly online. However, going forward, non-profits are likely to follow a hybrid fundraising event model using a blend of in-person and virtual.
  • Focus on Donor Acquisition & Retention - Non-profits would also focus more on donor retention in addition to donor acquisition, going forward. More than 70 percent of the donors give only once, to any organization, leaving non-profits with huge losses on their first investments on acquiring donors. The organizations therefore would focus on strategies for keeping individuals motivated for repeat and consistent donations. New technologies and tools for fundraising developed in the recent years are currently making it simpler for non-profits to enhance donor experiences for improved donor retention. The tools are allowing donors to easily access information about movement of their donations thus eliminating barriers to make repeat donations for the organization. It is now possible to share impact reports with donors. These reports demonstrate the work being carried out by the non-profits. However, non-profits are required to calculate their giver retention rate, time to time, for understanding effectiveness of strategies thus far and if any changes are needed for reaching the set goals. Finally, donor retention is achieved mainly through building strong relationships in which the donors are appreciated for their generosity. Appreciation increases donor retention rate and sustains success for fundraising of a non-profit. Non-profit fundraisers would also develop programs for monthly giving for creating for themselves a sustainable and predictable revenue stream. Younger donors are more attuned to such monthly giving because such giving can be managed easily and can be more impactful.
