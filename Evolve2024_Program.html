Despite economic uncertainties, CVC remains a vital element of corporate strategy, offering a low-risk, high-reward path to innovation. The expert panel provides valuable insights into how and why innovators should consider CVC as a strategic value.

Gen AI – LLMs, SLMs & Compressed Models

The generative AI market is projected to grow at over 40% CAGR, potentially surpassing $1 trillion by 2030, driven by the popularity of products like Google’s Bard and OpenAI’s ChatGPT. Generative AI, including Large Language Models (LLMs) and Small Language Models (SLMs), is revolutionizing sectors with diverse applications. LLMs, known for their extensive parameters and deep learning capabilities, generate high-quality text but require significant computational resources. SLMs, with fewer parameters, offer a resource-efficient alternative ideal for on-device applications. Emerging hybrid models combine the strengths of both, balancing large-scale data processing with efficiency. Advances in AI are enhancing model capabilities, addressing explainability, energy consumption, and domain-specific needs. Integrating Generative AI into business operations is crucial for maintaining competitiveness and innovation in the evolving market landscape.

Space Tech & Future Aviation

Session 27 - Panel

3:30 PM – 4:15 PM

Quantum Computing – Are We Prepared?

Quantum computing is poised to be a major technological shift, following the mainstream adoption of generative AI. Global VC investment in quantum computing reached $1.2 billion in 2023. Key trends include focusing on qubit quality and error correction, which are crucial for practical applications. Sectors such as finance, pharmaceuticals, and sustainability are exploring its potential, with significant impacts expected on data security and encryption standards. Increase in qubit count and coherence times in quantum devices and improving quantum algorithms will be vital for adoption. The transition from physical qubits to error-corrected logical qubits and increased global collaboration will mark significant progress. Quantum computing’s advancements could revolutionize industries, drive new applications, and require robust cybersecurity measures to mitigate emerging threats.
